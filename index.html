Whole Brain Paragraph

When I work with kids I really have to balance my brain and how I lead the kids. I love working with kids and it can be so much fun, but you also have to stop and realize that how you behave, the words you use, and how you guide them is how they are going to do the same things. I love to have fun and be animated when working with kids and let them have some free range. If we are in public at a museum I want to show them excitement and wonder and explore, but I also want to show them how we behave in public, how we respect others while we do what we enjoy, and I stop and help them learn what they are curious about. I help them learn how to do math, how to cook, respect, wander, how to use their resources, and the incredible impact they have on the world even as kids. I do this by setting rules in place, by giving them the extra push they need, with discipline, with play therapy, with attention and love.
